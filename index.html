Firmware Installer

Flashes the UV-K5 APRS firmware and configures it entirely in your browser over the programming cable — backup, wipe, flash, and set your callsign & position. No software to install.

Licensed amateurs only. This firmware transmits on the 2 m amateur band (144.800 MHz APRS). You need a valid amateur licence and must set your own callsign. Use at your own risk — flashing replaces the radio's firmware.

  1. Turn the radio on, then connect

    Power the radio ON normally (do not hold PTT). Plug the programming cable into the radio and this computer. Then connect:

  2. Back up your radio

    We save the full EEPROM and the factory calibration (0x1E00–0x1FFF, unique to your radio — irreplaceable). Keep these files safe.

  3. Wipe old settings

    The radio's current settings use a different layout that would confuse the new firmware, so we clear them (0x0000–0x1DFF). Calibration is preserved. Run this only after you've saved the backup above.

  4. Enter flash mode

    Turn the radio OFF. Then turn it ON while holding PTT — the flashlight LED lights up. Leave the cable plugged in. Then confirm:

  5. Flash the firmware

    This writes the APRS firmware (~59 KB). Do not unplug or power off during flashing. Desktop only — open this page on a computer to flash.

  6. Configure your station

    The radio rebooted into the new firmware. Enter your details — we write them straight into the radio.

  7. Done — how to use it

    Your radio is now an APRS station. Tune to 144.800 MHz, set menu APRS → ON to listen, and use TX to beacon. Incoming messages to your callsign pop up on screen (any key clears them). Menu group: APRS, Intv, Call, SSID, Loc, Cmnt, MsgTo, Msg, Send, TX. VFO/TX are limited to amateur bands.
